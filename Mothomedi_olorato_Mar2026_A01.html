PART A: Website Planning & Documentation

1. Website Specifications [10 Marks]

1.1 Website Objective & Goals

The Lumina Academy website serves as the official online presence for an IT and Cybersecurity training academy based in Botswana. The primary objectives are:

  • Showcase course offerings across Cybersecurity, Ethical Hacking, Network Administration, Cloud Computing, IT Support, and CompTIA Security+ preparation.
  • Provide prospective students with detailed program information including duration, difficulty level, and certification pathways.
  • Build trust through student testimonials, a training gallery, and a professional Coursera-inspired design that conveys credibility.
  • Facilitate student inquiries through a fully validated contact form with real-time input checking.
  • Offer free learning resources (ebooks, video tutorials, practice labs) that demonstrate value and attract potential enrollees.

1.2 Target Audience

Primary Audience: Age 18–35, located in Botswana and across Southern Africa. This group includes high school graduates seeking IT careers, university students supplementing their studies, and working professionals looking to upskill or certify. Technical aptitude spans from complete beginners (IT Support, Cybersecurity Fundamentals) to intermediate learners (Ethical Hacking, CCNA).

Secondary Audience: Organizations seeking workforce upskilling in cybersecurity, parents/guardians researching training options, and international students interested in African remote learning opportunities.

1.3 Type of Website

This is an Educational / E-Learning promotional website. It functions as a digital storefront for Lumina Academy, providing course catalogs, institutional information, learning resources, and a contact gateway. The design follows modern e-learning platform conventions inspired by Coursera.

2. Information Design & Taxonomy [5 Marks]

The information architecture follows a flat hierarchy with a persistent sticky navbar providing access to all primary pages from any location. The 3-click rule is maintained throughout — users can reach any content within 3 clicks from the homepage.

Site Structure

  • Home (index.html) — Hero with search, 6 category cards, 3 featured courses, testimonials
  • About (about.html) — Mission, story, core values, CTA to courses
  • Courses (courses.html) — 6 course cards with images, ratings, duration, badges
  • Gallery (gallery.html) — 9 training images with overlay captions
  • Resources (resources.html) — 3 cards (ebooks, videos, labs) with active links
  • Contact (contact.html) — Two-column: contact info + validated form, social links

All pages share a common sticky navbar and multi-column footer for consistent navigation.

7. Future Developments & Recommendations [3 Marks]

  1. Backend Integration: Add server-side processing (PHP, Node.js, or Python) for the contact form to send actual emails instead of relying solely on client-side JavaScript.
  2. Student Portal: Implement login/registration so enrolled students can access course materials, track progress, and communicate with instructors.
  3. E-Commerce: Integrate a payment gateway (PayPal, PayStack) to allow students to enroll and pay for courses directly through the website.
  4. LMS Features: Add a full Learning Management System with video hosting, quizzes, assignment submission, and progress tracking.
  5. Blog Section: Share cybersecurity news, tutorials, and industry insights to improve SEO and return visitor engagement.
  6. Performance: Implement image lazy loading, CSS/JS minification, and a CDN for faster global loading times.
  7. Accessibility: Improve WCAG compliance with ARIA labels, keyboard navigation, focus management, and screen reader support.
